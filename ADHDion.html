ADHD

A neurodevelopmental disorder characterised by hyperactivity,
impulsivity, and trouble focussing. It can be especially difficult
to maintain organisation, finish work, and control attention.

  • About 11.4% of U.S. children aged 3-17 have been diagnosed with ADHD, and 2.58% of adults continue to experience it into adulthood (CDC).
  • ADHD is linked to increased injury risk, poorer academic outcomes, and higher likelihood of risk-taking behaviors (e.g., substance use) among teens and adults (National Institute of Mental Health).
  • In U.S. adults (aged 18-44), the current prevalence of ADHD is 4.4%, with a lifetime prevalence of 8.1%(National Institute of Mental Health).
  • Around 33% of adults with ADHD take stimulant medications, but 71.5% reported difficulty obtaining prescriptions, and ~50% have used telehealth for care (CDC).
