Cosmic Calm FAQ

The Motivation Behind Our Project

What is this website?
Cosmic Calm is a space-themed mental health website, where each illness is represented by a unique planet. Our goal is to increase awareness of mental health through visuals, research, and interactivity.
What are some main functionalities?
Our website has interactive, fictional planets that represent different mental disorders. Users can explore information, take a quiz, and listen to carefully selected music.

Mental disorders are often generalized as negative, so our website combats that idea and provides users with a place where they can feel seen.

Additionally, we’ve implemented a custom cursor that changes when hovering over interactive items. In case a user isn’t sure if they can click on something, they can check the cursor.
What is the problem we are trying to solve and why?
Mental health is often misunderstood, stigmatized, or reduced to clinical terms, which makes it hard to emotionally connect with. We wanted to create a bridge between abstract struggles and tangible understanding.
What idea are we excited to explore?
What if mental illnesses were planets, each with their own ecosystems, weather, and lore? By turning psychological experiences into immersive worlds, we make them easier to explore, discuss, and empathize with.
What inspired us to start this project?
We kicked things off by focusing on what united us: a shared interest of mental health awareness. Our goal was to help users feel understood and seen. After brainstorming, we decided on a website that used metaphors to convey how it feels to experience various disorders.

Finally, here we are, presenting our work! We're extremely excited about getting to make a difference in the battle of mental health.
What makes this meaningful or important to us?
This matters to us because everyone deserves to feel seen. If users can walk away from this experience with something memorable, we’ve turned isolation into connection.
Who might use our product?
Teens & young adults (ages 15–30) exploring mental health, plus educators/therapists seeking creative tools. Gamers, sci-fi fans, and art-driven learners will love the format.
What problem can our website solve?
Mental health can feel isolating or hard to articulate. Our "planets" turn abstract struggles into immersive, relatable worlds, making self-reflection feel more like exploration than therapy.
What were some challenges along the way?
While creating the quiz page, it took several tries to make the autocheck algorithm work properly. As soon as the results were outputted onto the webpage, the form immediately refreshed, clearing the feedback. After reviewing the code, we realized that the “resultsDiv.innerHTML” line was inside the for loop, so we took it out.

Another problem that arose was related to the custom cursor. It didn’t appear on some pages, so we individually pasted the code onto all of the webpages.

There were also CSS issues with designing and arranging elements, like buttons and images. We had to go through a lot of trial and error in order to get to our ideal final product.
In what specific circumstances might people use our product?
  • For fun: Curiosity-driven exploration (like a game).
  • For learning: Teachers/therapists using it to spark discussions.
  • For self-discovery: Someone thinking, "Wait, does my brain feel like this stormy planet?"
Who do we hope this product will impact?
Anyone who’s ever felt alone in their struggles. By framing mental health as a universe to explore, not just a problem to fix. We help users feel seen, not misunderstood.
What were our main takeaways from this project?
  • Mental health is a sensitive topic. However, this only makes honest and open discussions about it even more important, and they can also be approached in engaging and creative ways.

  • Constant reflecting and research is key while developing and improving a project.

  • A lot of trial and error is needed in the web development process. No one can succeed in everything from the get-go.
